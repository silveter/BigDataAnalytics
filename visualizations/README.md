# Energy Data Visualizations

This folder contains visualizations generated from the global energy consumption and emissions analysis.

## Visualizations

### 1. Greenhouse Gas Emissions Trends (2000-2022)
**File:** `01_ghg_emissions_top10_countries.png`

Shows the historical trends of greenhouse gas emissions for the top 10 emitting countries from 2000 to 2022. This line chart helps identify which countries are the largest contributors to global emissions and how their emissions have changed over time.

### 2. Regional Emissions Comparison
**File:** `02_ghg_emissions_by_region.png`

Compares total greenhouse gas emissions across major world regions (Africa, Asia, Europe, North America, South America, and Oceania). This horizontal bar chart provides a regional perspective on emission distribution.

### 3. Renewable Energy Share Growth
**File:** `03_renewable_share_growth.png`

Tracks the growth of renewable energy share over time (2000-2022) for the top 10 countries leading in renewable energy adoption. This visualization highlights successful transitions to cleaner energy sources.

### 4. Renewable Energy Impact on Emissions
**File:** `04_renewable_vs_emissions.png`

Scatter plot showing the relationship between renewable energy share and greenhouse gas emissions for top emitting countries. This helps visualize whether increased renewable adoption correlates with reduced emissions.

### 5. Global Energy Consumption Forecast
**File:** `05_arima_forecast_global_energy.png`

ARIMA time series model projection of global primary energy consumption for the next 10 years, based on historical World data. Shows both historical trends and forecasted future consumption.

## Data Source

All visualizations are generated from the Our World in Data (OWID) energy dataset, which includes comprehensive global energy statistics from 1900 to 2023.

## Generated By

These visualizations were created using:
- **Matplotlib** for static PNG image generation
- **Pandas** for data processing and analysis
- **Statsmodels** for ARIMA forecasting (chart 5)

For interactive versions or to regenerate these charts, see the `EDA_in_Databricks` notebook in the repository root.
